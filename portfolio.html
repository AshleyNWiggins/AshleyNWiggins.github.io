Artist Statment

As someone who has worked with 3D modeling, animation, paper and pencil, fabric and ink, clay, sculpture, and paint on canvas, I have become fascinated with how art is perceived and how the medium affects our understanding of the work. In my art, I focus on prompting a change in perspective. I hope to open a portal to a new understanding and appreciation of the world around us by subverting expectations.





One of a Kind:
A Textile Design Collection

Previous Next


One of a Kind: A Textile Design Collection is a textile design collection created using both hand-printed and digitally printed designs on fabric. Textile design is something that we encounter in everyday life. We look at these designs without a second thought, never wondering what effort went into creating these patterns. They are designs we see on our clothes, our bags, and our beds. One of a Kind utilizes our familiarity with this medium to explore the artistic side of textile design. By creating designs with strong focal points, the viewer can focus on the work as art rather than a simple pattern. Additionally, by using familiar subject matters in the patterns, Wiggins draws her viewers into the details of the designs, opening up a whole new level of appreciation for the time and effort put into each aspect of the collection.





Soho Loft

Previous Next


This project is a digital 3D model replica of a real life New York apartment interior, meaning that the model was created with the aid of photographs of a real apartment that I had never visited or seen in person. The intention of the work is to look as realistic as possible, while only using basic Maya modeling and texturing techniques. I am happy with the replication of complex shapes and details of the apartment as well as my ability to find and create high quality textures for those shapes. I think I did well paying attention to the details of the photos that were available to me, which allowed me to create a detailed model. There are definitely a few things that could use improvement, like adding certain details to my models and texture.





Custom Controller



This is a custom controller to be used to play video game ROMs on a computer, such as Pokémon Pinball for the Nintendo GameBoy Advanced and Mario Kart for the N64. It uses simple contact point buttons connected to the Arduino Leonardo, which has the capability to mimic a computer keyboard and mouse. This controller is meant to be played with two people at a time, splitting the controls between both people to try to create a collaborative situation in the single player game, though it can be used with one player or even three or more players. It has two tethered “pokéball” buttons on either side of the controller, which is held by each of the two players. I’m really proud of how this project turned out as a whole. The only thing I would do differently is change the length of the tethers so that the players could step further back from the screen for more space. This project is extra special to me because I was able to show it to other students and see them really enjoy my creation just like I hoped they would.





NERD!



This video is gameplay of a puzzle game I created. The game was created using Processing, a java based language, and mostly found images. The game itself consists of references to popular games, TV shows, and movies. In order to complete the game, you must follow the directions given on screen for the four different typs of puzzles. The only image I made was the "Eeveengers" poster in the "find the pokeball" puzzle, and I wish I had created more of the images myself rather than relying on found images from the Internet. I also wish I had added background music and sound effects to the game. Still, I'm really happy with how this project turned out as a whole. This was the first game I coded entirely on my own. I think it's a fun little game that works well.





Legend of a Hero

This video is a parody of tropes of the adventure genre of both video games and movies. It is meant to be a comical exploration of the often times ridiculous plot and logic of children's adventure stories, in which a princess must be saved by a prophesied legendary warrior who is still but a boy, waiting to become a hero. The aspect of this project that I'm happiest about is actually the script and the story, since I cannot take responsibility for the music. I have never written a comedy script or story before so I think this one turned out pretty nicely. Although, I think a few more revisions could've made vast improvements. Considering that it is a 22 minute long video, that was made in probably less than a week, frame by frame, I don't think the animation's half bad. Though I will say there is a lot of room for improvement. My biggest nemesis with this project was the timeframe. I think a lot of these improvements I'm talking about would have vanished with more time. I had a really fun time making this.
