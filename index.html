Ashley Wiggins

About Ashley

Ashley Wiggins is a digital artist who primarily works with 3-D modeling, animation, and interactive design with hopes of one day branching into video game design. She is currently pursuing a Bachelors of Arts degree in Digital Arts with minors in Computer Science and Studio Art at Stetson University in Deland, Florida. Ashley has coding experience in Processing, Java, and HTML and has worked with a variety of digital arts softwares such as Photoshop, Illustrator, Dreamweaver, After Effects, Maya, Mudbox, and Zbrush. She has also used Arduino software in conjunction with Processing in the creation and interaction of simple games.

Senior Goals

My goals for senior year are to expand on the video game I will be starting this summer, and, time permitting, perhaps even build a custom arcade cabinet for the game. Both my interactivity and 3D modeling sequences have helped in shaping my understanding of how to do this. In my senior year, I hope to take sculpting classes to boost my understanding of 3D modeling in the real world, which should translate to a better understanding of modeling in the digital realm. None of the graduating seniors have projects exactly like this, but there are several projects with similar aspects to the one I hope to accomplish. For example, the animations have elements I need to consider the creation of the game, like characters design, sound design, art style, animation style. Meanwhile, the interactivity projects are about creating a unique and interesting way of interacting with a digital experience in the real world. Something I need to consider if I am going to build the custom arcade cabinet. I am very interested in game design as a career post-Stetson, and so I am hoping that this kind of project on my resume would make me more desirable to such companies.
